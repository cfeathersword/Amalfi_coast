Cetara — The Anchovy Village
Italy's anchovy capital and one of the most authentic stops on the coast. Anchor in the bay and snorkel the clear shallows around the tower — good visibility over rock and posidonia grass. 🤿 Suggested ashore: dinghy in for a stroll through the village and pick up bottles of colatura di alici to take home.
16:00Overnight
🌙 Dinner on Board — Amalfi Marina
Stern-to at Marina Coppola, Amalfi. Optional: evening stroll up to the Cathedral of Sant'Andrea and aperitivo on the waterfront as the cliffs turn gold. Dinner served on board. Overnight in the marina.

Monday 22 June
Amalfi → Furore → Positano → Marina del Cantone
▾
09:30~45 min
Fiordo di Furore — Swim Stop
One of the most dramatic spots on the entire coast — a narrow fjord sliced vertically into the cliffs, invisible until you're almost upon it. Anchor just off the entrance and swim in through the gorge to the tiny pebble beach inside. 🤿 The rocky walls and clear water make for good snorkelling too. Go early — it gets crowded by late morning.
11:00Afternoon
Positano — Anchor
Anchor off the beach. Swim off the boat and relax through the afternoon. Suggested ashore: dinghy in and explore Via dei Mulini's boutiques and the narrow lanes.

⚑ Optional: Path of the Gods — Sentiero degli Dei
One of the great walks of southern Italy, tracing a ridge 500m above the sea with unbroken views along the entire Amalfi Coast. Difficulty: Easy to Moderate — the classic direction (Bomerano → Nocelle) runs almost entirely downhill on well-maintained stone paths. No scrambling or technical sections. Total distance 7.5km, 2.5–3 hrs walking. Cumulative descent ≈650m.

Directions from the boat: Dinghy ashore by 08:00. Taxi from Positano waterfront to Bomerano / Agerola (≈30 min, ≈€35–45 — arrange the night before if possible). Walk east to west along the waymarked trail, with the sea always in front of you. The path passes through ancient terraces and abandoned farmhouses before arriving at the hamlet of Nocelle. From Nocelle, descend the 1,800 stone steps into Positano (≈45 min, steep but clearly signed) and dinghy back to the boat for a well-earned swim. Aim to start walking no later than 09:00 — late June heat on the open ridge can be intense after midday.

What to bring: Trail shoes or sturdy trainers · 2L water per person · sun hat & sunscreen · light snack · cash for the taxi.
16:30Overnight
🍽️ Dinner Ashore — Marina del Cantone, Nerano
Anchor in this sheltered turquoise bay inside the Punta Campanella Marine Reserve. Dinner ashore tonight — this is the birthplace of spaghetti alla Nerano (zucchini, aged provola, basil) and it must be eaten here. Try Lo Scoglio or Maria Grazia. Overnight on anchor — one of the finest anchorages on the coast.

Blue Grotto
Arrive before 9am — queues build fast in late June and the grotto closes if westerly swell exceeds ~50cm. Transfer to the mandatory small rowboats from your catamaran. Worth every minute inside.

Anchor off Marina Grande. Motor the dinghy around to the Faraglioni rock stacks and snorkel the base of the arches — excellent marine life shelters in the rock. 🤿 Crystal clear. Suggested ashore: funicular up to Capri town and the Piazzetta, Gardens of Augustus for the views.

🍽️ Dinner Ashore — Procida
The most authentic and unhurried island in the Gulf of Naples. Berth in the marina. Dinner ashore tonight — walk to the pastel-stacked Marina Corricella and eat fresh-caught seafood at one of the quayside trattorias. Gorgonia or La Lampara are perennial favourites. Overnight in Procida marina.

Ischia — Baia di San Montano
Short hop to Ischia for a mid-morning swim stop in the stunning turquoise shallows of Baia di San Montano. 🤿 Good snorkelling over the sandy bottom and volcanic rock outcrops. Optional: visit the Poseidon or Negombo thermal gardens, or the medieval Aragonese Castle.
12:30~1 hr
Li Galli — The Sirens' Islands
A small private archipelago known in antiquity as home to Homer's Sirens, once owned by Rudolf Nureyev. Anchor off for a swim and snorkel in extraordinary clear water. 🤿 Rocky seabed with excellent visibility — often possible to see the bottom in 15m+.

Anchor off Positano beach for the final night. Dinner ashore tonight — dinghy in and splurge on a terrace table with a full bay view. Next2 or Il Ritrovo are superb. A perfect last evening on the water. Overnight on anchor.

Captain's Notes

Late June Tips

🌊
Blue Grotto
Arrive before 9am. The grotto closes entirely when westerly swell exceeds ~50cm — have a backup plan. Queues can be 2hrs+ by mid-morning in peak season.
⚓
Anchorages
Late June is high season. Arrive at popular bays (Positano, Marina del Cantone) before noon to secure a good spot — they fill up fast.
🌬️
Wind Pattern
Expect light mornings with afternoon Maestrale (NW wind) building to 10–15kts. Plan longer passages for the morning. The return leg east can be a lovely reach.
🛒
Provisioning
Stock up thoroughly in Salerno before departure. Prices on Capri and Ischia in particular are significantly higher than the mainland.
🐟
Must Eat
Colatura di alici in Cetara. Spaghetti alla Nerano in Nerano. Fresh-caught fish on Procida. Don't miss the thermal springs on Ischia's Maronti beach.
🏊
Marine Reserve
Punta Campanella Marine Reserve (near Nerano) requires anchoring in designated areas. The visibility is exceptional — bring snorkelling gear.
